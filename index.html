Must Visit Destinations

From historical cities to natural splendours, come see the best of Lucknow

Husainabad Clock Tower

Husainabad Clock Tower was constructed in 1881 to mark the arrival of Sir George Couper, the first lieutenant governor of United Province of Avadh.

Know More
Bada Imambara

Bara Imambara, also known as Asfi Mosque is an imambara complex built by Asaf-ud-Daula, Nawab of Awadh in 1784.

Know More
Rumi Darwaza

The Rumi Darwaza, also known as the Turkish Gate, is an imposing gateway which was built under the patronage of Nawab Asaf-Ud-Daula in 1784.

Know More
Chota Imambara

Also known as Imambara Hussainabad Mubarak, is an imposing monument built for Shia Muslims, by Muhammad Ali Shah, the Nawab of Awadh in 1838.

Know More
Jama Masjid

Built with "Lakhauri" bricks and plastered with lime, Jama Masjid was built by Majesty King Mohammad Ali Shah Bahadur with the intention to surpass the Jama Masjid at Delhi.

Know More
Residency Lucknow

The Residency, also called as the British Residency and Residency Complex, served as the residence for the British Resident General who was a representative in the court of the Nawab.

Know More
Indira Gandhi Planeterium

Situated at Suraj kund Park, it is a novel attraction of the city. The Saturn shaped building of the planetarium is unique of its kind.

Know More
Kukrail Picnic Spot

One of the favourite picnic spots in Lucknow, Kukrail houses a deer park and one of the nurseries of the endangered species of crocodiles in India.
